I built and shipped [Block Puzzle Master](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.horizngames.blockpuzzlemaster) to the Google Play Store in 30 days. Here's the exact workflow I used.

## Week 1: Concept & Prototyping

### Day 1-2: Validate the Idea

Before writing code, I validated the concept:

- Researched similar games on Google Play
- Analyzed reviews to understand what players want
- Identified gaps — what existing games were missing

The goal was simple: build a puzzle game that feels polished and fun, not just another clone.

### Day 3-5: Core Gameplay Prototype

Built the minimum viable game loop in Unity:

- Block placement mechanics
- Grid system and collision detection
- Line clearing logic
- Basic scoring

No UI, no polish — just the core mechanic. If the gameplay isn't fun at this stage, no amount of polish will fix it.

### Day 6-7: Playtest & Iterate

Played the prototype myself (a lot) and had a few friends test it. Asked:

- Is it intuitive without instructions?
- Is it satisfying to play?
- Would you play this again?

Based on feedback, I adjusted the block rotation speed and grid size. Small changes, big impact.

## Week 2: Building the Game

### Day 8-10: UI/UX Design

Created the visual identity:

- Clean, minimal UI
- Color scheme that doesn't fatigue the eyes
- Smooth animations for feedback (blocks snapping into place, lines clearing)

Used Unity's UI system and tweaked until it felt right. Good UI is invisible — you don't notice it, but you'd notice if it was bad.

### Day 11-13: Game Features

Added essential features:

- Main menu and settings
- Sound effects and background music (with mute option)
- High score tracking
- Pause and resume functionality

Kept features minimal. The goal was to ship, not to build every feature I could think of.

### Day 14: Mobile Optimization

Tested on different devices:

- Optimized frame rate for low-end phones
- Adjusted touch controls for different screen sizes
- Reduced memory usage to prevent crashes

Mobile optimization isn't optional — if your game lags or crashes, users uninstall.

## Week 3: Testing & Iteration

### Day 15-18: Bug Fixing

Played the game obsessively, looking for bugs:

- Edge cases in block placement
- Score calculation errors
- UI elements not scaling properly
- Memory leaks

Fixed bugs as I found them. Kept a list, prioritized by severity.

### Day 19-21: Balance & Polish

Adjusted difficulty curve:

- Are players getting bored too quickly?
- Are they hitting frustration too early?
- Is the progression satisfying?

Added juice — small animations, particle effects, screen shake when clearing lines. These details make the game feel alive.

## Week 4: Launch Preparation

### Day 22-24: Google Play Console Setup

Created the Google Play listing:

- **App name** — clear and searchable
- **Description** — highlighted what makes the game fun
- **Screenshots** — showing actual gameplay, not just the logo
- **Icon** — recognizable at small sizes

Wrote the privacy policy and set up app details (age rating, content description, etc.).

### Day 25-26: Build & Upload

Generated the Android App Bundle (AAB) in Unity:

- Configured build settings for release
- Signed the app with the keystore
- Uploaded to Google Play Console

First upload failed — wrong versioning. Fixed it, re-uploaded. Second attempt worked.

### Day 27-28: Internal Testing

Used Google Play's internal testing track:

- Shared with a few testers
- Collected feedback on performance and bugs
- Made final tweaks

Internal testing catches issues before the public sees them.

### Day 29-30: Launch

Submitted for review. Google Play review took about 24 hours. Once approved, the game went live.

Shared the launch on social media, messaged friends, and monitored the first installs.

## App Store Submission Tips

### What Google Play Looks For

- Clear app description
- Privacy policy (required)
- Accurate age rating and content declarations
- No copyrighted assets (music, graphics, etc.)
- App follows [Google Play policies](https://play.google.com/about/developer-content-policy/)

### Common Rejections

- Missing privacy policy
- Inaccurate content rating
- Crash on startup (test thoroughly!)
- Misleading screenshots or descriptions

### Approval Time

Google Play review typically takes 1-3 days. If rejected, fix the issue and resubmit. Don't panic — rejections happen.

## Marketing Your First Game

### Pre-Launch

- Build anticipation on social media
- Share development progress (screenshots, videos)
- Create a landing page or website

### Post-Launch

- **App Store Optimization (ASO)** — optimize title, description, and keywords
- **Social media** — share the launch, gameplay clips, user testimonials
- **Community engagement** — respond to reviews, listen to feedback
- **Update regularly** — players notice when a game is actively maintained

### What Worked

- Sharing gameplay clips on TikTok and Instagram Reels
- Responding to every review (positive or negative)
- Updating the game based on user feedback

### What Didn't Work

- Paid ads (too expensive for an indie game with no revenue yet)
- Spamming communities with "check out my game" posts

## Lessons Learned

**Ship fast.** 30 days forced me to cut features and focus on what mattered. A finished game beats a perfect idea.

**Playtest early.** If the core loop isn't fun, nothing else matters.

**Mobile optimization is critical.** Your game has to work on cheap phones, not just flagships.

**ASO matters.** A good listing gets more organic installs.

**Respond to reviews.** Users appreciate when developers listen.

## The Numbers

After 30 days and launch:

- Development time: ~100 hours
- Tools used: Unity, CapCut (for promo videos), Canva (for assets)
- Initial installs: modest, but growing
