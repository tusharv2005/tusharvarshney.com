I've always been drawn to building things people interact with — not just look at, but actually use, play, and enjoy. That's why I started [Horizn Games](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.horizngames.blockpuzzlemaster).

## The Vision

Horizn Games isn't just about making games. It's about creating experiences that feel right — polished, intuitive, and genuinely enjoyable. I wanted to build a studio that focuses on quality over quantity, where every release is something I'd personally play and recommend.

The name "Horizn" is inspired by the event horizon of a black hole — that point of no return. It's a metaphor for creating games so engaging that players can't help but keep playing.

## Market Research & Finding the Niche

Before diving in, I spent time analyzing the mobile gaming landscape. The market is crowded, but there are gaps — opportunities where polish, design, and user experience can make a real difference.

I looked at:

- **User retention patterns** — what makes people come back?
- **Monetization strategies** that feel fair, not predatory
- **Design trends** in successful indie games
- **Technical constraints** of mobile platforms

The goal was to find a niche where I could compete not with budget, but with execution and attention to detail. That led me to focus on casual puzzle games with a modern twist — accessible, satisfying, and built for mobile-first experiences.

## Building Games as a Solo Founder

Running a game studio solo means wearing every hat — game designer, developer, marketer, analyst, and support. It's challenging, but it's also liberating. Every decision is intentional. Every feature ships because it improves the experience, not because a committee approved it.

Here's what the workflow looks like:

1. **Design & Prototyping** — Start with the core loop. What's fun? What feels right?
2. **Development in Unity** — Build the game with performance and scalability in mind
3. **Testing & Iteration** — Play it, break it, fix it, polish it
4. **Launch & Monitor** — Ship to Google Play Store, gather feedback, iterate based on real usage
5. **Marketing & Growth** — Organic discovery, app store optimization, community building

The first major release — [Block Puzzle Master](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.horizngames.blockpuzzlemaster) — was a validation that the approach works. Real users, real engagement, real feedback.

## The Tech Stack

Unity is the backbone of development, with a focus on:

- **Mobile optimization** — smooth performance across a wide range of devices
- **Scalable architecture** — clean code that can grow with the studio
- **Analytics integration** — understanding player behavior to improve experiences
- **Monetization frameworks** — fair, non-intrusive revenue models

Every tool and framework choice is made with long-term sustainability in mind.

## Future Roadmap

Horizn Games is just getting started. The roadmap includes:

- **New game releases** — exploring different genres while maintaining quality standards
- **Community building** — creating spaces for players to connect and share experiences
- **Cross-platform expansion** — bringing games to iOS, web, and beyond
- **Open development** — sharing the journey, challenges, and wins with other indie builders

The goal is to build a studio that's known not for how many games it releases, but for how good those games feel to play.

## Why Games?

Games are the ultimate test of product design. They have to be intuitive without instructions, engaging without coercion, and rewarding without manipulation. If you can build a great game, you can build great products in any category.

For me, Horizn Games is where creativity, engineering, and user experience intersect. It's where I get to build something that brings a small moment of joy to someone's day. And that's worth every late night and tough decision.
